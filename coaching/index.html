// Questions people actually ask

Before you apply

Do I need a gym?

No. Every program lists a gym version and a home/dumbbell swap for every exercise. A pair of adjustable dumbbells covers you; a full gym just gives us more tools.

I've never really trained. Is this for me?

Yes — every track has a Level 1 built for under a year of consistent training, and your first weeks are deliberately conservative. The application asks about your experience so you start exactly where you are, not where Instagram thinks you should be.

Are there video calls?

Coached runs on weekly written check-ins with video feedback from me — thorough, but asynchronous. All-In adds a live weekly 30-minute call. Both get direct messaging.

Why a 3-month minimum?

Because that's the honest minimum for visible body composition change. Anyone promising it faster is selling you something. Your rate never rises while you stay — early clients keep their price for life.

What about nutrition — do I get a meal plan?

Better: you get your numbers and the skill to hit them with food you actually like. Rigid meal plans break the first weekend they meet real life. Note: I provide fitness education, not medical nutrition therapy — for clinical conditions I'll refer you to the right professional.

What happens after I apply?

I personally read your application within 24 hours. If we're a fit, you get a payment link; your program lands within 48 hours of that. If we're not, I'll say so honestly and point you somewhere better.
